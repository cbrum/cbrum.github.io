About me

My name is Cecilia Brum, I’m a UI/UX Designer who lives in Montevideo, Uruguay.

I am veteran in the field, I have been in it for almost 10 years now, and what a decade it’s been!

At first, web designer meant to design User interfaces (UI) for the web, and building them with html and css, for developers to work the backend and add functionality.

Over time the field has changed quite a bit. Building and designing became more complex in themselves, and through the process part of my colleagues headed for the "building" side, and became what's now called: frontend developers. I... went in the other direction, which at the begining was focused just on UI. As time went by, UI became just an aspect of a bigger whole. Other parts of the puzzle emerged, like usability, interaction and accessibility, expanding it all into broader concept: User Experience (UX).

I was there to be part of such changes in the industry and our daily lives. Since the begning, I’ve learnt a lot about working with with UI, html and css, I’ve even coded for a while. I got to learn from amazingly talented people from many backgrounds and places, even when being a teacher I learnt a lot from my students.  In recent years, mobile phones opened a new world of possibility and challenge, and diving into UX was inevitable for me.

A lot has happened over the last decade, and I’m happy I’m a part of it.  I love what I do, I love co-creating experiences, always eager to improve myself and curious to know what comes next, in a world that was bearly glimpsed when I first got into it.
